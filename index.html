Research Areas

Development of adaptive systems for personalized rehabilitation

My research focuses on developing intelligent and adaptive human–robot interaction systems, with applications in robot-aided rehabilitation, occupational safety, and healthcare. I design multimodal monitoring pipelines (physiological, kinematic, behavioral data), build computational models to estimate the user’s physical, cognitive and emotional state, and integrate adaptive control and automated planning to personalize robotic assistance.

Multimodal Systems

Development of monitoring platforms that integrate physiological, kinematic and behavioral data to obtain a complete understanding of the user's state during robotic therapy.

Physiological monitoring Kinematic analysis Behavioral assessment

Psychophysiological State Estimation

Implementation of advanced computational models for real-time estimation of the patient's mental and physical state, using machine learning techniques and analysis of biological signals.

Machine Learning Biological signals Predictive models

Adaptive Control

Integration of adaptive control systems with automated planning to personalize rehabilitative interventions based on individual patient responses.

AI Planning Real-time control Personalization
