Research Activities

My research activities focus on developing technologies and methods of personalized robotic rehabilitation, enabled by multimodal monitoring and artificial intelligence, impacting exoskeletons, prosthetics and ergonomics, and healthcare robotics.

Research Areas

Three main areas summarizing my recent contributions. For each: goals, methods, impact and representative works from my publications.

Personalized Robotic Rehabilitation & Adaptive HRI

Planning and goal reasoning for personalized sessions; adaptive impedance control; online reinforcement learning; cognitive and social robotic architectures to promote engagement and behavior change.

Goal Reasoning Adaptive Impedance Reinforcement Learning Cognitive/Social Robotics

Multimodal Monitoring & Psychophysiological Assessment

Estimation of workload/affect and psychophysiological state with physiological sensors; data-driven methods (fuzzy logic, feature selection); smart garments and contactless vital signs measurement.

Multimodal Sensing Affective/Workload Fuzzy Logic Smart Garments

Assistive Systems & Ergonomics (exoskeletons, prosthetics, healthcare)

Gait analysis with deep learning for lower-limb exoskeletons; biomechanical and physiological effects of passive upper-limb exoskeletons; semiautonomous prosthetic control; hospital robotics and navigation benchmarking.

Exoskeletons (UL/LL) Prosthetics Ergonomics Hospital Robotics

Collaboration & Technology Transfer

I am interested in projects with clinics, rehabilitation centers, and industrial partners for clinical validation, co-design of interfaces, benchmarking, and technology transfer.
