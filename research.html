Research

Cognitive architectures for intelligent robotic rehabilitation

Research Areas

Development of adaptive systems for personalized rehabilitation

My research activity focuses on the development of advanced cognitive architectures for robotic systems used in rehabilitation. The main objective is to create intelligent systems that dynamically adapt to the individual characteristics of each patient, thus improving the effectiveness and accessibility of rehabilitative treatments. to the individual characteristics of each patient, thus improving the effectiveness and accessibility of rehabilitative treatments.

Multimodal Systems

Development of monitoring platforms that integrate physiological, kinematic and behavioral data to obtain a complete understanding of the user's state during robotic therapy.

Physiological monitoring Kinematic analysis Behavioral assessment

Psychophysiological State Estimation

Implementation of advanced computational models for real-time estimation of the patient's mental and physical state, using machine learning techniques and analysis of biological signals.

Machine Learning Biological signals Predictive models

Adaptive Control

Integration of adaptive control systems with automated planning to personalize rehabilitative interventions based on individual patient responses.

AI Planning Real-time control Personalization

Clinical Validation

Conducting clinical studies to validate the effectiveness of developed robotic solutions, collaborating with medical teams and hospital facilities.

Clinical trials Medical collaborations Outcome evaluation
